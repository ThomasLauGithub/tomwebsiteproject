1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business Information technology has become the bread and butter of most modern businesses. It is regarded as a driver for innovation and innovation is the path to business success. It has been said that innovation in business has the same impact that steam had on the industrial revolution. [2] Let’s consider an example: For many companies, email is the main means of communication between employees, suppliers and customers. Email was one of the early drivers of the Internet, providing a simple and inexpensive means to communicate. Over the years, several other communications tools have also evolved, allowing staff to communicate using live chat systems, online meeting tools and video-conferencing systems. [3]

1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

( what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills? ) In order to complete digital projects successfully a variety of resources can be used such as Trello. Trello is an online based resource tool which is known for its ease of use and popularity amongst software developers. [4] It is particularly useful for creating task lists and for the application of the widely recognised Kanban system, a visual system employed for managing work as it transitions through the stages of ‘to do’, ‘doing’, and ‘done’. The goal of Kanban is to identify potential bottlenecks of project work and fix them so that the work continues to flow cost-effectively towards the point of completion. [5] Particularly useful for software developers is GitHub. Moreover, Git is a revision control system, a tool to manage source code history. GitHub is a hosting service for Git repositories. It is worthy of note that they are not the same thing: Git is the tool, GitHub is the service for projects that use Git. [6]

In addition, personal professional skills are of great value when applied to digital projects. For example, time management skills are essential for leading a project successfully. The ability to work in a team cannot be overlooked as much of the work of digital projects revolve around good collaboration.

1.4 Risks that may impact digital projects

(Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?) With projects in general, many, many risks can be listed which may range from the project scope being ill-defined to it having insufficient funding, from stakeholder conflict to the project incurring legal liability. [7] Digital projects, however, are uniquely subject to certain key risks. They suffer from the threat of potential cyber attacks, targeted hacking, or inaccessibility due to technological faults. Digital projects are notoriously difficult to estimate and schedule, often suffer from inflation of requirements as they progress, employee turnover and poor productivity. For example, if the scope of a digital project is not clearly defined, or is too broad, a sense of urgency may be lacking and so work may tend to expand to fill the time available. [8]

1.5 Selecting and using IT systems and software

( how do you select the right kind of technology for the job? ) One must begin by thinking critically about what the project in question requires. It is often beneficial to write a list of basic needs in order to complete a project, or even just to get it up and running. Some examples of this are a stable internet connection, sufficiently powerful laptops/ macbooks, a platform for testing the project, and a way to present the project product upon completion. Candidate options should be listed and research performed on each item, noting the search hints provided about refining web searches [9]. competing choices of a particular item, a simple pros and cons list can help clarify which option would make for the best choice. If further analysis is required, a S.W.O.T. analysis can be performed on the various options. [10] However, sometimes the choice of IT systems and software is dictated by the umbrella company or company headquarters if they are keen to limit spending. In that case choice is limited simply to what IT systems and software resources are already available.
